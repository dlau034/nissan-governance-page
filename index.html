1 · Scope

Does your app need validation?

All Nissan apps issued by Nissan companies and created for commercial use must go through validation before release.

Validation required

Consumer-facing Nissan mobile apps

All Nissan apps created for end users

Campaign apps

Timeline flexibility available for campaign delivery

Dealer support apps provided by Nissan

e.g. NCAR, Nissan Catalog

Out of scope

Dealer apps for internal use

Not formally validated

Internal dealership apps

Not formally validated

Third-party dealer service apps

e.g. local dealer apps created by third parties

For out-of-scope apps: While formal validation isn't required, we recommend following MDS guidelines and distributing them to relevant teams.

2 · Form Contents

What the request form captures

Here's what you'll be asked when you submit a validation request. Having these ready will speed up the process.

Project information

App name, market, contact, and current stage of project.

  • App name and market/region
  • Project contact information
  • New app or update status
  • Current project stage

Service selection

Engagement level and validation services you will need.

  • Engagement level selection
  • Concept validation needs
  • UX/UI validation requirements
  • Naming & icon support

Dates & milestones

Design readiness, build dates, and target release.

  • Design review readiness date
  • Test build availability
  • App Store submission date
  • Target release date

Naming & trademark

Naming options and potential IP considerations.

  • Full Name options (store listing)
  • Display Name options (home screen)
  • Known trademark concerns
  • Submit early for legal checks

Designs & materials

Figma links, wireframes, supporting documents.

  • Figma links or file uploads
  • Service blueprints & user flows
  • PDF, PNG, PPTX, XLS (max 25MB)
  • Links to Confluence/Jira/drives

5 · Validation Process

What we validate

Each service has a clear purpose, a simple process, and defined deliverables.

App owner responsibility
Validator responsibility
External / legal
SERVICE WHEN WHAT TO SUBMIT PROCESS
Concept Validation Does the app concept meet customer and business needs? At the beginning of the project, before design starts. Service Blueprint, concept overview, early naming/icon/UX thinking
App Owner → Mobile Advisory Board
→ App Owner
Naming Validation Does the name fit the Nissan app family and meet IP requirements? When naming options are ready. Provide both Full Name (App Store / Google Play) and Display Name (on-device). Naming options (Full + Display), Naming validation request sheet

*Trademark checks handled by AV4 Local Legal — plan ahead, can take several weeks
App Owner → Mobile Advisory Board
→ AV4 Local Legal
App Icon Validation Does the icon align with the App Icon Design System? When options are ready and naming has been validated. App Icon Validation Request sheet, icon design options (if available)

*Trademark costs covered by app owner team
App Owner → Mobile Advisory Board
→ AV4 Local Legal
UX / UI Validation Are the experience and interface aligned with global guidelines and MDS? UX: When wireframes are ready. UI: After UX validation is complete. UX wireframes & user flows, user requirements, UI designs (for UI validation)
App Owner → Mobile Advisory Board
→ App Owner
Build Review & Final Check Is the built app ready to go live? When a test build or test account is ready, before submission to App Store / Google Play. Test account or build access, Final Check sheet
Mobile Advisory Board → App Owner
→ Mobile Advisory Board
Post-Release Audit Review of the live app after release to verify quality in production. After the app has been released and is live in the App Store / Google Play. Live app vs. validated designs, any issues introduced during release, store listing accuracy
Mobile Advisory Board → App Owner
